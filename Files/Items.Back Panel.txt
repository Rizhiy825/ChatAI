Drag the back panel in the base.
You can enter the desired dimensions for the panel.
In the settings window you will find more settings to optimize your panel.
Name
: Enter a name for your panel.
Setback
: Enter a setback, which will be subtracted from the outer base backline.
Thickness
: Enter the thickness of the panel
Overlap
: Enter for every side how much the back panel is supposed to overlap in the corpus panels.
You can extend the panel over the base volume.
Keep in mind that you can not add any panels outside the base volume even if other panels are extended over the base volume.
Back panel rebating: Enter if the back panel is supposed to get a rabbet. This may be important if you want to use a 19 mm back panel and don´t want mill the whole material depth in the corpus panels.
Front
: Enter the depth if the milling is supposed on the frontside of the back panel.
Back
: Enter the depth if the milling is supposed on the backside of the back panel.
Machine on CNC
: Tick the box if you want to machine the back panel on CNC.
Tool Number
: Select the tool number which you want to use for the machining.
Custom Feedrate
: Tick the box when you want to define the feed rate by your own.
Feedrate
: Enter the feed rate you want to use.
Carcass Rebate Extension: You can define a desired airgap between back panel and corpus panels when you open the dropdown menu.
Air Gap Width
: Enter the desired air gap in the width between back panel and corpus panels. The gap will be considered for all sides.
Air Gap Depth
: Enter the desired air gap in the depth between back panel and corpus panels. The gap will be considered for all sides.
Top Left
: Enter how much the groove is supposed to be extended on the top in the left corpus panel.
Top Right
: Enter how much the groove is supposed to be extended on the top in the right corpus panel.
Bottom Left
: Enter how much the groove is supposed to be extended on the bottom in the left corpus panel.
Bottom Right
: Enter how much the groove is supposed to be extended on the bottom of the right corpus panel.
Skewed Edges
: If you have angled panels which fit to other panels you can trim or extend the panel if needed.
Pick Point
: If you add a component or split a panel by WoodWOP you can choose which part of your panel will be needed.
Export
: Select a machining for inserting the back panel to the corpus panels by opening the drop down menu.
Machining Type
: Select a machining for inserting the back panel to the corpus panels.
Tool Number
: Select the tool you want to use.
Mill Diameter
: Enter the diameter of the tool.
Mill Feedrate
: Enter the desired feed rate of the tool.
Machine Corners
: Tick the box if you want clean the corners with a mill.

: Enter the desired angle for your panel. You can enter an angle of-89° to 89°.
Setback
: Give the item a setback that reduces the volume for all subsequent items that are dragged in.
The setback entered is subtracted from the front edge of the item and limits the volume in order to assign a general setback to the subsequent items.
Items that are drawn in after an item with a setback, are automatically calculated by the volume with the reduced setback and therefore have the value 0 for the setback.
